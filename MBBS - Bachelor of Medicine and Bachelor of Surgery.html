Course Overview

The MBBS (Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery) is a comprehensive 5.5-year undergraduate program that trains students to become qualified medical doctors. The program integrates a 4.5-year academic curriculum with a compulsory 1-year clinical internship. MBBS equips students with in-depth theoretical knowledge and hands-on clinical skills essential for diagnosing and treating a wide range of health conditions. It plays a vital role in strengthening healthcare systems and improving public health outcomes globally.

Who Should Pursue This Course?
  • Students passionate about healthcare and helping others
  • Interest in Biology, Chemistry, and Life Sciences
  • Aptitude for problem-solving and analytical thinking
  • Willingness to work in high-pressure environments
  • Aspiration to pursue careers in clinical practice, medical research, public health, or medical education
Eligibility Criteria
UG
  • 10+2 (Higher Secondary) in Science stream with Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Biotechnology
  • Minimum 50% aggregate marks (General category), 40% (Reserved categories)
  • Minimum age: 17 years
PG
  • MBBS degree from a recognized institution
  • Internship completion certificate
  • NEET-PG qualification required for MD/MS programs
Others
  • English proficiency required for abroad programs
  • Age eligibility per individual country/university regulations
Entrance Exams & Admission Process
UG
  • NEET-UG (Mandatory for all MBBS admissions in India)
PG
  • NEET-PG (Mandatory for MD/MS admissions in India)
Others
  • Direct Admission in select private colleges under Management Quota
Course Duration
  • UG: 5.5 years (4.5 years academic + 1-year internship)
  • PG (MD/MS): 2–3 years
  • PhD: 2–3 years (optional after PG)

FAQs & Student Queries
Q1. How difficult is it to get into MBBS?

Ans. Admission to MBBS is highly competitive in India; cracking NEET-UG with a good score is essential.

Q2. Can I switch to other healthcare careers after MBBS?

Ans. Yes, MBBS graduates can explore public health, hospital administration, research, and teaching roles.

Q3. What are the PG options after MBBS?

Ans. MD/MS in various specialties, DNB programs, MPH (Public Health), MBA in Hospital Administration.

Q4. What is the workload like for MBBS students and doctors?

Ans. The academic load is heavy, and the profession demands long working hours and emergency duty readiness.

Q5. Is an MBBS degree from abroad valid in India?

Ans. Yes, provided the foreign MBBS degree is recognized by the NMC (India), and the graduate clears the FMGE (Foreign Medical Graduate Examination).
